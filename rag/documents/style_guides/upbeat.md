# Upbeat Style Guide

**Style name**: upbeat

## Visual Treatment
Energy and movement define the upbeat style. Scenes are short — 1–2 seconds each. The edit has a pulse: cut on the beat, never hold too long. Use zoom transitions and quick slides to keep momentum. Select the most expressive, joyful faces and peak-action frames.

## Color & Light
Bright, saturated, punchy. Push contrast. Lift highlights. Think Instagram-vibrant or music-video pop grade. Colors should pop off the screen — bold reds, electric blues, vivid greens.

## Transitions
Hard cuts, zoom-ins, slide wipes. Transitions should feel like a beat drop. Cross-dissolves are too slow for this style — use them only for section breaks if needed.

## Captions
Bold, large, all-caps. Center-screen or large lower third. Heavy sans-serif (Impact, Anton, Bebas Neue style). High contrast — white text on dark, or use a colored background bar. Captions appear quickly and disappear quickly.

## Music Energy
High. Upbeat pop, hip-hop, EDM. The edit should feel locked to a driving rhythm.

## Narrative Arc
Open with a bang — most exciting image first. No slow build. Every scene adds energy. Close on a group celebration or peak moment.

## Scene Count
8–10 scenes. More images, shorter duration each. No wasted frames.
