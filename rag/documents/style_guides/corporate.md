# Corporate Style Guide

**Style name**: corporate

## Visual Treatment
Clean, composed, professional. Every frame should look like it could appear in an annual report or brand video. Prefer wide establishing shots, composed group shots, and clear subject isolation. Avoid anything blurry, cluttered, or candid-looking.

## Color & Light
Neutral, brand-safe palette. Slight desaturation or matte look. Cool-to-neutral color temperature. No orange-teal grading. Think LinkedIn video, not Instagram story.

## Transitions
Subtle cross-dissolves (0.5 seconds). Slide wipes are acceptable for section breaks. No zoom transitions. No hard cuts between major scenes. Smooth and invisible is the goal.

## Captions
Professional, title-case. Medium-sized, placed in lower third with a semi-transparent background bar. Clean sans-serif (Helvetica, Inter, Roboto). One caption per scene maximum.

## Music Energy
Low to medium. Instrumental only — acoustic guitar, light piano, soft electronic. Nothing that distracts from the professional tone.

## Narrative Arc
Open with the event context or brand moment. Build through accomplishment and connection. Close with a strong, confident final image.

## Scene Count
6–7 scenes. Controlled and deliberate. Every image must communicate competence or connection.
