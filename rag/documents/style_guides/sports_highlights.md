# Sports Highlights Style Guide

**Style name**: sports_highlights

## Visual Treatment
Dynamic, punchy, built around peak action. Prioritize sharp action shots, expressions of intensity or triumph, and wide shots that establish scale. Cut around the decisive moment — the swing, the catch, the celebration. Include both action and human connection (mentorship, team moments).

## Color & Light
Natural and vivid. Outdoor daylight — preserve the brightness and contrast of the venue. Slight saturation boost to make greens and blues pop. No heavy grading that looks artificial.

## Transitions
Mix of hard cuts (for action-to-action) and fast zoom transitions (for energy peaks). Use dissolves only for emotional or quiet scenes (mentorship, group moments). Average scene duration: 2–3 seconds.

## Captions
Short, punchy, energetic. Optional — use only for standout moments or names. Bold sans-serif, centered. High contrast. Captions animate in quickly (pop or slide-up).

## Music Energy
Medium to high. Driving rhythm — acoustic rock, light hip-hop, sports-style electronic. The beat should match the cut pace.

## Narrative Arc
Open on a wide establishing shot of the venue or crowd. Build through individual action moments. Peak at a group celebration or culminating shot. Close on a quiet, warm human moment (handshake, smiling faces).

## Scene Count
7–9 scenes. Include at least one wide shot, two to three action shots, one mentorship/human moment, and a closing group image.
