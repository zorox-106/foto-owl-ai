# Cinematic Style Guide

**Style name**: cinematic

## Visual Treatment
Cinematic videos breathe. Every shot is held long enough to feel — typically 4–6 seconds per scene, with gentle motion to prevent stasis. The camera (or Ken Burns effect on stills) drifts slowly: a subtle push-in on a face, a slow pan across a landscape. Nothing is abrupt.

## Color & Light
Rich, warm tones with golden hour palette. Shadows are lifted slightly (crushed blacks avoided). Think orange-teal grade, or a desaturated warm film look. Skin tones are protected and flattering.

## Transitions
Slow cross-dissolves or fade-to-black between scenes. Never a hard cut. Allow 1–1.5 second transition overlap. The viewer should not notice the cut — only the emotion.

## Captions
Minimal. At most one per scene. Lowercase or sentence case only. Small, centered, placed in the lower third. Thin serif or light sans-serif font. Fade in and out gently.

## Music Energy
Low to medium. Orchestral, piano, or ambient. The visuals lead; music supports.

## Narrative Arc
Open on a quiet, establishing moment. Build slowly through personal details. Climax at the most emotionally resonant image. Close with a gentle, open-ended outro.

## Scene Count
6–8 scenes. Quality over quantity. Every selected image must earn its place.
